How it works
From chaotic group chat to clear progress in minutes.
Create tasks with real due dates, priorities, and tags, collaborate in chat, and track fair contribution scores across every project group.
1) Plan the work

Capture tasks with assignee, due date, priority, and course-specific tags so no detail is lost.

2) Collaborate live

Discuss in context with each team and keep updates tied to the right project space.

3) Prove contribution

Monitor completion and contribution score so everyone sees who is helping move the project forward.

Why GroupSync
Built different, on purpose.
Every other tool was built for work teams or general productivity. GroupSync was built from the ground up for how students actually collaborate.
⚖️

Fairness Tracking

GroupSync logs individual contributions so every member — and optionally, the instructor — can see who is carrying the load. No more free riders.

✓ vs. Notion, Trello, Slack — none of them track this
🎓

Academic Deadline Logic

Smart reminders that understand semester timing — syncs with course weeks, not calendar months. Alerts escalate as due dates approach.

✓ vs. Google Tasks — no academic context
🧠

Low Cognitive Load UI

Designed with Nielsen's heuristics — minimal clicks to create tasks, join groups, or check progress. Built for students already managing multiple courses.

✓ vs. Asana / Monday — way too complex for students
📱

True Mobile-First

Full feature parity on mobile. Not a stripped-down app — everything works the same on your phone as on desktop, because students live on their phones.

✓ vs. most LMS tools — desktop-only or clunky mobile
🔒

Student Autonomy, Not Surveillance

Instructor visibility is opt-in and limited by design. Transparency lives within the team first — not a monitoring tool for professors.

✓ vs. LMS integrations that feel like surveillance
♿

WCAG 2.1 AA Accessible

High-contrast colors, keyboard navigability, and screen reader compatibility baked in from day one — not bolted on after launch.

✓ vs. most student tools that ignore accessibility
